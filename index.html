THE CHARACTERS OF THE PROPHET MUHAMMAD

  1. Outstanding Qualities of the Holy Prophet

    1. Honesty and Truthfulness

      • All the people in Makkah used to call him Al-Sidiq (The thruthful) and Al-Amin (The trustworthy).
      • Hazrat Khadija married him becuase of his honesty and trustworthiness.
      • Even Abu Jahl and Abu Sufyan admitted that Muhammad was not a liar.

    2. Humility and Modesty

      • He disliked if anyone stood up when he entered a room, for he never wished to be a treated as a ruler.
      • Not only himself lived a simple and modest life, but also taught his followers to be humble, kind and courteous to all.
      • He said "Every religion has a distinctive quality, and the distinctive quality of Islam is modesty." (Ibn Majah)
      • "And walk not on earth with conceit and arrogance." (17:37)

    3. Fulfillment of Promises and Honoring the Covenant

      • The Holy Prophet always fulfilled his promises, so much so that people used to keep their valuebles with him for safe keeping.
      • He disallowed two of his companions to participate in the Battle of Badr because they had promised to the Makkans that they would not fight on the side of the Muslims.
      • "And fulfill the covenant, for the covenant shall be questioned about." (17:34)

    4. Forgiving Others' offences

      • When he conquered Makkah, the city where he and his followers were subjected to rusthless torture, humiliation, insults, boycott, and persecution for 13 years, he pardoned everyone.
      • When the Holy Prophet was maltreated at Taif, Angel Jibrail offered to crush them between the mountains, but he pardoned them.
      • He forgave Wehshi who kiled Hamza in the Battle of Uhud, and Hind who had torn out and chewed Hamza's liver.
      • "Let them forgive and passover (the offence). Do you not desire that Allah should forgive you?" (24:22)

    5. Sincerity and dedication

      • The Holy Prophet was always sincere in his mission. He never doubted it and never sought any reward or favor for it.
      • The leaders of Quraish gave him tempations of wealth, kingship, marriage with the most beautiful lady of Makkah but he refused them all telling that he had a divine mission to accomplish.
      • "And no reward do you ask of them for this: it is no less than a message for all creatures." (12:104)

    6. Love, Kindness and Compassion to All People

      • He took great interest in the welfare of all people and had great compassion for the ones in trouble.
      • The Holy Prophet said to Hazrat Ayesha "Never turn away any needy man from your door empty-handed... Love the poor, bring them near to you and Allah will bring you near to Him."
      • "O Messenger of Allah! It is a great mercy of Allah that you are gentle and kind towards them, for had you been harsh and hard-hearted, they would all have broken away from you." (3:159)

  2. Behavior of the Holy Prophet Towards Others

    1. Behavior Towards Companions

      • He was always very considerate and helpful towards all of his companions, each one had a direct rapport with him.
      • He not only gave individual attention to all of them but was also concerned about the overall welfare of each member of the Muslim community.
      • When he was asked for anything he never said no to anyone.

    2. Behavior Towards Enemies

      • He never took revenge from anyone, so much so that he forgave even his bitterest enemies.
      • When he conquered Makkah, the city where he and his followers were subjected to rusthless torture, humiliation, insults, boycott, and persecution for 13 years, he pardoned everyone.
      • When the Holy Prophet was maltreated at Taif, Angel Jibrail offered to crush them between the mountains, but he pardoned them.
      • He forgave Wehshi who kiled Hamza in the Battle of Uhud, and Hind who had torn out and chewed Hamza's liver.

    3. Behavior Towards Women

      • His behavior towards women was especially very compassionate.
      • He gave them honour, dignity, and respect.
      • He motivated the women to acquire knowledge and become the heart of their families.

    4. Behavior Towards People in General

      • He was very loving and affectionate towards all people, be they believers or non-Muslims.
      • He freed 70 prisoners of war in Badr, more than 100 of Banu Mustaliq, and 600 from the Battle of Hunain.
      • He was often worried and grieved for people, even before his mission of prophethood.

    5. Behavior Towards the Orphans, the Poor and the Needy

      • The Holy Prophet was exceptionally kind and friendly to the orphans, the poor and the destitute, and commanded his companions to show utmost regard and kindness to them.
      • The Holy Prophet was himself an orphan, so he knew very well what orphanage meant and to what torture and grill one is subjected if the father and the mother are not alive in one's early life.
      • Whenever the Holy Prophet saw any needy person in distress, he would gather the Muslims to help their brothers.
      • "Therefore, treat not the orphan with harshness, nor repulse the beggar." (93:9-10)
      • "See you one who denies religion? He is the one who repulses the orphan (with harshness) and encourages not the feeding of the poor." (107:1-3)

    6. Behavior Towards Children

      • He was so considerable that if he heard any baby crying, he would shorten the prayer.
      • He never differentiated between the children of believers and non-believers.
      • The Holy Prophet love his own children very much. He always showed special love and consideration to his daughter Fatima and her sons, Hassan and Hussain.

    7. Behavior Towards Slaves

      • He treated them with the utmost love and kindness and always spoke to them in a friendly manner.
      • He set his own slave Zaid free, adopted him as his son and married him to Zainab.
      • Bilal, a negro slave, who was released by Abu Bakr, was appointed Muazzan at Masjid Nabawi by the Holy Prophet.
      • The Holy Prophet himself freed 63 slaves and so did many of his companions.
      • "The freeing of slaves is an act of great virtue in the sight of Allah." (90:12-13)

    8. Behavior Towards Workers

      • He always praised their contribution to the growing wealth and power of the society.
      • He himself had been a worker in his boyhood and had gone through all the sufferings and deprivations that the working class people have to face.
      • He never liked other people to be doing work while he was just stting watching, but always preferred to share the work.
      • The Holy Prophet did all he could to raise the dignity of labor as well as their living standard.

    9. Behavior Towards Animals

      • The Holy Prophet was very kind and gentle to animals and took great care to see that they were fed properly, supplied with water and not put to work for long hours.
      • He was also very considerate to all kinds of birds and taught his companions not to disturb or destroy the habitations of insects.
      • The Holy Prophet said: "When you cut an animal's throat, sharpen your knife to give the animal as little pain as possible."
